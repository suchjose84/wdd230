Target Audience

The target audience of Panahon Weather web site is described below:

  • Who: High School Students, Teachers, Employees from all industries, College Students, and other people who are interested to know the weather. The target audience of Panahon Weather specifically people who have and know how to use smart phones or computers.
  • Age: 14 years old and above.
  • Income: Income levels will be between (Php)10,000 and above monthly.
  • Motivation: Improve weather awareness. Improve disaster preparedness and survivability.

Content Architecture

Content List

This section describes how each of the scenarios identified in the previous section will be answered by providing a well organized content.

Scenario 1: When is the typhoon going to hit?

  • A page containing a report of the weather forecast which includes a 5-day forecast and by 3-hourly interval reports whenever there is a typhoon.
  • A satellite timelapse showing the speed and direction of typhoons. The representation will also show forecasted direction at a specific time which is purely calculated based on the collected data.

Scenario 2: Where is the typhoon going?

  • Same as scenario 1, a weather report will be provided regarding the position and speed of the typhoon. A calculated 5-day weather forecast will be provided which will include the direction of the storm at a given time.

Scenario 3: What is the typhoon signal right now?

  • Whenever there is a typhoon, there will be a by 3-hourly typhoon status report which will include the typhoon signal numbers.
  • These warning signals range from signals #1 to #5, #1 being the weakest at 30-60 KPH and #5 being the strongest of more than 220KPH wind speeds.
  • Please see the website for more information.

Scenario 4: Is there a low pressure area(LPA) again?

  • By accessing the Panahon Weather website, you can easily view satellite images of the weather in your area, so it is easy to see if there is a storm coming. It is in the homepage.
  • There will also be an alert on the homepage if there is a typhoon. If there isn't, a "No Active Tropical Cyclone" alert would be seen.

Scenario 5: There's a typhoon. Do we have school today?

  • The by 3-hourly report will also include public announcements from the local government to inform the education sector and the public of school and work cancellations depending on the storm signal.
